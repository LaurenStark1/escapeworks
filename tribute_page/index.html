A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada

His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada
  • 1896 - Born in Calcutta, India
  • 1933 - Gets initiated into renounced order of life
  • 1939 - Receives the title "Bhaktivedanta"
  • 1944 - Begins publishing "Back to Godhead" magazine, an English fortnightly magazine presenting the teachings of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu
  • 1950 - Accepts vanaprastha ashrama and goes to live in Vrindavan
  • 1952 - Attempts to set up organized spiritual activities in the central Indian city of Jhansi, where he starts "The League of Devotees", only to see the organization collapse two years later
  • 1959 - formally enters sannyasa ashrama from Bhakti Prajnan Keshava at his Keshavaji Gaudiya Math in Mathura and is given the name Bhaktivedanta Swami
  • 1965 - Receives a vision from his spiritual master in which he was instructed to take the message of Krishna consciousness to the Western world. He accepts this mission and set sail for America, arriving in New York City
  • 1966 - Establishes International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) with the first center in New York on 26 Second Avenue
  • 1967 - establishes a second center, in San Francisco
  • 1968 - Devotees open a temple in London, England
  • 1970 - Makes first of several visits to Kenya. Also directs his attention especially to India, with the hope of turning India back toward its original spiritual sensibilities.
    - Establishes GBC.
  • 1971 - Visits Moscow, which marks the beginning of Krishna consciousness in Soviet Union
  • 1977 - Dies at the age of 81
